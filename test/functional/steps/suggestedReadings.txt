Additional readings

Listed below are the additional readings that accompany each of the modules in this course. Unfortunately we are unable to provide these readings so we suggest you search for them on the internet or at your local library:
Module one

John Berger, Ways of Seeing, London: Penguin, 1972.
Jaynie Anderson, Tiepolo’s Cleopatra, Melbourne: Macmillan, 2005 - We follow the formal analysis of The Banquet of Cleopatra presented by Professor Anderson.
Diana E. E. Kleiner, “Chapter 7 Living the Inimitable Life”, Cleopatra and Rome, Cambridge, Mass.: Harvard University Press, 2005, pp. 102-118 - This chapter is a good summary of Cleopatra’s relationship with Marc Antony.
Diana E.E. Kleiner, “Chapter 9 “Queen of Kings”: Cleopatra Thea Neotera”, Cleopatra and Rome, Cambridge, Mass: Harvard University Press, 2005, pp. 135-156 - This chapter is about Cleopatra’s iconography in Egyptian art as Pharaoh.
Elaine Fantham et al., Women in the Classical World: Image and Text, New York: Oxford University Press, 1994, pp. 136-139 - This short section from a chapter on “The Hellenistic Period: Women in a Cosmopolitan World” includes some portraiture of Cleopatra, plus a genealogy chart of her royal ancestors.
Svetlana Alpers and Michael Baxandall, Tiepolo and the Pictorial Intelligence, New Haven: Yale University Press, 1995.

G.J Barker-Benfield, The Culture of Sensibility, Sex and Society in Eighteenth Century Britain, Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 1992.
Martin Myrone, Bodybuilding: Reforming Masculinities in British Art, 1750-1810 New Haven; London: Yale University Press for the Paul Mellon Centre for Studies in British Art, 2006.
